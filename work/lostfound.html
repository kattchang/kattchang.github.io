LOST & FOUND
A documentation of process through the lens of the camera.


OBJECTIVE

The objective for this piece was to create a compendium compile from photos taken over the course and explain our design process. Photos included are my coffee filters taken in different perspectives and dried flowers assembled so the shadows look like the alphabet.

PROCESS



Research To make sure the compendium revealed my process, I set out to create a different way of folding the compendium. I knew that I wanted it to reveal each photo and passage slowly to bring the reader into my work. To get a better direction, I made a moodboard of inspiration (pictures are from the wonderful website of Pinterest!) to document my ideas.

Mockup The design of the compendium went through many iterations before it was perfected to the level I wanted. With each iteration, I changed different aspects to see how they integrated with the content before settling with the final version. However before going into the final print production, I needed to make sure everything was in the right places so I made a full scale mockup.

Branding I decided to take a further step and made a cover design with an identity that represented my photography journey. Instead of using shapes or symbols, I chose to use the words "Lost" and "Found" because they echoed my sentiments throughout the whole process. In order to put more emphasis on the feelings of the words, the words "lost" were grouped in a square with a small opening the represented my fustration of being stuck. The word "found" was singled out to prove the point of breaking free from the group.

FINAL THOUGHTS

Lost & Found is meant to bring the audience into my story through hands-on experience. By opening the flaps one by one, photos and words are revealed for the audience to take in. Once the whole piece is opened up the words "Lost", "Frustrated", "Inspired", and "Found" can be seen.
